# 🚲 Cyclistic Bike-Share: Google Data Analytics Capstone

## 📋 Project Overview
This case study is the final capstone project for the **Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate**. 

**The Goal:** Analyze last 12 months trip data from Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company in Chicago, to identify trends in how "Casual" riders and "Annual" members use the service differently. The ultimate goal is to design a marketing strategy to convert casual riders into loyal annual members.

---

## 🛠️ Tools Used
* **Data Cleaning:** Python (pandas , numpy)
* **Data Analysis:** Python (Pandas , numpy)
* **Visualization:** Python (seaborn ,matplotlob)
* **Report:** Microsoft Word & PDF & Google Docs

## 📈 The Analysis Process
1.  **Ask:** Defined the business task—how do annual members and casual riders use Cyclistic bikes differently?
2.  **Prepare:** Used Cyclistic’s historical trip data (around 5.5+ million rows) ensuring data integrity and privacy.
3.  **Process:** Cleaned the data by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and creating new columns for 'ride_length' and 'day_of_week'.
4.  **Analyze:** Performed descriptive analysis to find trends, averages, and usage patterns.
5.  **Share:** Created visualizations to communicate findings effectively to stakeholders.
6.  **Act:** Provided data-driven recommendations for the marketing team.

---

## 📈 Key Findings & Insights

### 1. User Segmentation & Volume
* **Market Share:** Annual members account for **65.1%** of total rides, while casual riders make up **35.9%**.
* **The Weekday Divide:** During weekdays, members outnumber casual riders by **2:1**. However, during the weekends, the volume between both groups becomes almost equal, suggesting casual riders use the service primarily for weekend leisure.

### 2. Temporal Trends (When they ride)
* **Peak Days:** Member activity peaks on **Thursdays** (consistent with commuting), whereas Casual riders peak on **Saturdays** (consistent with tourism/leisure).
* **Daily Peaks:** Both groups see their highest volume in the evening between **4:00 PM and 7:00 PM** (16:00–19:00).
* **Ride Duration:** Despite having lower total volume, **Casual riders have significantly longer average ride times** compared to members.

### 3. Geographic Distribution (Where they ride)
* **Casual Hotspot:** The #1 station for casual riders is **DuSable Lake Shore Dr & Monroe St** (~30k riders), a major tourist and leisure destination by the lake.
* **Member Hotspot:** The #1 station for members is **Kingsbury St & Kinzie St** (~28k riders), an area located in a heavy business/office district.

### ❄️ Seasonal Discovery
* **Weather Impact:** There is a drastic drop in ridership during the **Winter months (Nov, Dec, Jan, Feb)** compared to the Summer peak. This highlights that the bike-share service is highly weather-dependent in Chicago.
## 🚀 Final Recommendations
1.  **Seasonal Promotions:** Launch summer-only membership packages to target casual riders during peak leisure months.
2.  **Weekend Incentives:** Offer member-only "Weekend Benefits" to encourage casual weekend riders to subscribe.
3.  **Commuter Rewards:** Highlight the cost-savings of annual memberships for frequent weekday riders.
